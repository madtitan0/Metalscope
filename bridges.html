Steel Bridges & Infrastructure Engineering Solutions

Business Vertical / Bridges & Infrastructure

CONNECTING INDIA

Steel bridges represent one of the most technically demanding and socially impactful applications of structural engineering. Their design demands an integrated understanding of structural mechanics, material behaviour, fabrication constraints, erection methodology, and long-term durability — all of which must be balanced against programme, budget, and site constraints. MetalScope India brings this multi-disciplinary expertise to every bridge project we undertake.

MetalScope specializes in the complete project lifecycle for steel bridges and infrastructure — from structural engineering, fabrication, project management, to site erection. Our design engineers work to IRS, IRC, and IS codes as well as international references including AASHTO and Eurocode, enabling us to serve both Indian Railways and highway infrastructure clients with equal confidence. We use high-performance weather-resistant steel (Cor-Ten / WR-grade) where appropriate to minimize maintenance requirements over the bridge's service life.

Our fabrication facility in Pondicherry is equipped with heavy plate processing lines, submerged arc welding systems, large-format CNC drilling and cutting equipment, and a dedicated shot-blasting and painting bay capable of handling bridge girders up to 30 m in length. All bridge fabrication is executed under third-party inspection and is fully traceable to mill certificates and weld procedure qualification records.

Bridges & Infrastructure
Advantages of Steel Bridges

WHY STEEL BRIDGES

Steel bridges offer a unique combination of structural performance, speed of erection, and long-term durability that no other material can match.

Decreased Structural Weight
Steel's high strength-to-weight ratio results in bridge superstructures that are significantly lighter than equivalent concrete designs.
Lighter superstructure reduces pier and abutment loads, enabling more slender and economical substructure designs.
Reduced self-weight allows longer spans between supports, minimizing the number of intermediate piers required in river or road crossings.
Faster Erection
Prefabricated steel girders and trusses can be launched or craned into position rapidly, minimizing traffic or rail disruption during construction.
No in-situ concrete curing periods — once steel is in position and bolted/welded, the structure is immediately load-bearing.
Night-time and weekend erection windows are easily achievable with steel, enabling bridges over busy roads and rail lines to be erected with minimal service interruption.
Lighter Foundations
The reduced dead load of a steel superstructure directly translates to smaller and shallower foundation systems, lowering geotechnical risk and cost.
On sites with difficult ground conditions, the weight savings of steel can be decisive in achieving a viable structural solution.
Structural Efficiency
Plate girder and box girder sections can be optimized with variable depth and flange thickness, placing material only where structural demand is highest.
High-strength steel grades (S355, S460) enable slender profiles that reduce wind loading, improve hydraulic clearance, and minimize visual impact.
Composite action between steel girders and reinforced concrete decks substantially increases stiffness and reduces deflections under live load.
Long-Term Durability
Modern weathering steel grades form a stable patina that actively resists further corrosion, dramatically reducing the frequency and cost of maintenance repainting.
Steel bridges are readily inspectable — all structural members are visible and accessible for periodic inspection, fatigue assessment, and targeted repair.
Damaged or worn components can be individually replaced without removing the entire superstructure, extending service life at low cost.
Environmental Benefits
Steel is 100% recyclable and retains its full structural value when reprocessed — bridge steel has among the highest end-of-life recovery rates of any construction material.
Prefabrication eliminates on-site concrete batching, reducing water consumption, waste generation, and carbon emissions associated with conventional bridge construction.
Lighter foundations reduce the volume of concrete and reinforcement required, further lowering the embodied carbon footprint of the overall structure.
Faster Construction
The parallel fabrication and site preparation workflow inherent to steel bridge projects compresses overall delivery schedules by 30–50% compared to conventional cast-in-place concrete construction.
Architectural Aesthetics
Steel's formability enables elegant arch, cable-stayed, and truss profiles that enhance the visual character of the surrounding landscape and urban environment.
Slender profiles and graceful curves create landmarks that stand as enduring symbols of engineering ambition and civic pride.
Bridge Typologies

BRIDGE TYPES WE BUILD

From railway over-bridges to landmark marine arch structures — MetalScope fabricates and erects every category of steel bridge.

Road Over Bridge

ROB structures carrying road traffic over railway lines — RDSO and RITES compliant fabrication with full traceability.

Road Under Bridge

RUB solutions enabling road passage beneath existing rail embankments — minimal traffic disruption during erection.

Pedestrian Bridge / FOB

Foot Over Bridges for railway stations, urban intersections, and educational campuses — designed for high pedestrian volumes.

Flyover

Multi-span elevated highway structures with composite steel-concrete decks for fast-track urban traffic decongestion projects.

Network Marine Arch

Landmark arch bridges with network hanger systems for river crossings and signature infrastructure — complex geometry fabricated with precision.
